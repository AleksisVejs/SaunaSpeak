# SaunaSpeak — Filming Scripts (native speaker on camera)

12 clips, batched by location — shootable in two afternoons. Companion to
[marketing-assets.md](marketing-assets.md); every Finnish line is from the lesson DB,
so the app can show the same sentence when viewers arrive.

## Ground rules (read once, applies to every clip)

- **Vertical 9:16**, phone at eye level, arm's length or on a ledge. Natural light, window/sun facing the face.
- **3 takes of everything.** Leave 2 seconds of silence before and after each take — needed for editing.
- **Deadpan is the brand.** Finnish deadpan delivery straight into the lens IS the joke. No selling, no smiling on cue — the flatter, the funnier.
- All English hooks/captions are added in edit (CapCut auto-captions). The friend only speaks Finnish and acts. Nothing needs to be memorized — one line at a time, cut between.
- Every clip ends the same way: friend looks at camera, small nod or shrug. That's the outro; CTA text goes on top in edit.

## LOCATION A — Kitchen or coffee table at home (4 clips)

### A1. "Your textbook lied to you"
**Scene:** sitting at kitchen table, a textbook (any thick book works) open in front of him.
**Shot:** static, chest-up.

1. Reads from the book, stiff and over-articulated, like a 1970s language tape:
   > **"Minä olen... Puhutko sinä englantia?"**
2. Looks up at camera. Beat. Closes the book slowly. Pushes it out of frame.
3. Leans in, normal voice, fast and casual:
   > **"Mä oon. Puhuksä englantia?"**
4. Tiny shrug. Hold 2s.

**Edit:** hook text "Your textbook lied to you" over beat 1; ❌/✅ captions on the two versions; CTA over the shrug.

### A2. The maybe ladder
**Scene:** same table, coffee cup in hand.
**Shot:** static, chest-up. Each word is its own take — cut between them jump-cut style.

Deliver five ways of saying "maybe", each with escalating reluctance:

1. > **"Ehkä."** — neutral
2. > **"Kai."** — small shrug
3. > **"Varmaan."** — looking away
4. > **"Bussi tais just mennä."** — resigned, checking imaginary watch
5. > **"Nii kai sit."** — deep sigh first, maximum reluctance, sip of coffee after

**Edit:** hook "Finns never just say 'maybe'"; EN caption under each ("I guess… / probably… / suppose so, then").

### A3. Grandma is "it"
**Scene:** on the phone (or pretending), warm and genuine — the ONE clip where he smiles.
**Shot:** static, chest-up.

1. Ends a phone call warmly: "Moikka mummo!" — lowers phone.
2. To camera, fond:
   > **"Mun mummo soittaa mulle joka päivä."**
3. Beat. Still fond:
   > **"Se on ihana."**
4. Deadpan look at camera — as if nothing strange was said. Hold 3s.

**Edit:** hook "In spoken Finnish, your grandma is 'it'"; highlight **SE** in red when spoken; caption "hän ❌ → se ✅ — not rude, just Finnish".

### A4. The kiitos economy
**Scene:** table; someone off-screen (you) hands him things.
**Shot:** static, chest-up. Three micro-scenes.

1. Handed a pen, formal nod: > **"Kiitos."**
2. Handed a coffee, casual: > **"Kiitti."**
3. Handed a cinnamon bun, genuinely moved, hand on chest: > **"Kiitoksia."**

**Edit:** captions "formal / friends / when it's korvapuusti"; hook "How Finns actually say thank you".

## LOCATION B — Street / bus stop (3 clips)

### B1. The bus stop test
**Scene:** at an actual bus stop, waiting.
**Shot:** static, waist-up, street behind.

1. To camera, SLOW textbook Finnish, over-enunciated:
   > **"En minä tiedä, milloin se tulee."**
2. Beat. Then the same thing at full street speed, mumbled, looking down the road:
   > **"Emmä tiiä millon se tulee."**
3. Bus arrives (or doesn't). Shrug.

**Edit:** hook "2 years of Finnish. Understood nothing."; caption the slow version fully, the fast version appears as a blur then resolves; punch "Same sentence. You were taught the slow one."

### B2. One-word questions
**Scene:** walking toward camera (you walk backwards filming), or static against a wall.
**Shot:** energy clip — quick cuts between each question.

Rapid fire, each straight to lens, half-second pause between:

1. > **"Puhuksä?"**
2. > **"Tuuksä?"**
3. > **"Haluutsä?"**
4. > **"Lähetsä?"**
5. Then, full speed, as one breath: > **"Tuuksä mukaan?"** — beckoning gesture, walks off.

**Edit:** hook "Finns squeeze entire questions into one word"; show the long form melting into the short form above each ("Puhutko sinä → Puhuksä").

### B3. Number nicknames
**Scene:** anywhere outside; he checks his phone/watch.
**Shot:** static, chest-up.

1. Checks phone, groans quietly, to camera:
   > **"Mä herään seiskalta."**
2. Counts on fingers, deadpan, rhythm like a countdown:
   > **"Seiska. Kasi. Ysi."**
3. Beat. > **"Viiskytviis."** — shrug, pockets the phone.

**Edit:** hook "Finns have nicknames for numbers"; captions pair each with the textbook form (seitsemän → seiska…); punch "You learned the legal name. Finns use the nickname."

## LOCATION C — Café (2 clips)

### C1. Ordering coffee, textbook vs reality
**Scene:** at the counter or a table with a menu. Friend plays BOTH customers — change jacket/beanie between takes.
**Shot:** two static takes, same framing, cut side by side or back-to-back in edit.

Take 1 — "the exchange student", stiff, holding a phrasebook:
> **"Anteeksi. Minä ottaisin yhden kahvin, kiitos."**

Take 2 — the local, not looking up from phone:
> **"Moi, mä otan kahvin. Ja yks iso limu, kiitos."**
…then reacting to the till, mild pain: > **"Se tekee kymmenen euroo."** → **"Mä maksan kortilla."**

**Edit:** hook "POV: ordering coffee in Helsinki"; split screen or hard cut; punch "One of these gets answered in English."

### C2. Sticker shock
**Scene:** table, receipt in hand (any long receipt).
**Shot:** close-up start on the receipt, tilt up to face.

1. Reads the receipt. Freezes.
2. > **"Sata euroo?!"** — the loudest he gets all shoot
3. Beat. Quietly, to himself, staring into distance:
   > **"Ei oo totta."**
4. Drinks the coffee anyway. Hold.

**Edit:** hook "The first Finnish phrase you'll actually need"; zoom punch-in on beat 2; caption "€100?! …unbelievable."

## LOCATION D — Evening / bar or living room lamp light (1 clip)

### D1. The one-drink lie
**Scene:** warm evening light, one drink on the table. Jacket on — about to leave.
**Shot:** static, chest-up.

1. Phone buzzes. Reads it. Looks at camera. Long pause.
2. > **"Otetaanks yhet?"** — said like a man who knows exactly how this ends
3. Hard cut: same table, jacket off, tie/beanie crooked, several empty glasses (or bottles) around. Checks phone: it's late.
4. > **"Mun pitää mennä himaan."** — stands up, doesn't move. Hold.

**Edit:** hook "The most dangerous sentence in Finnish"; caption on beat 2: "'yhet' — literally: shall we take ONES. It's plural."; clock overlay 21:00 → 02:30; punch "It's grammatically never one drink."

## LOCATION E — Sauna / mökki / lake (2 clips — July gold, shoot these if at all possible)

### E1. Sauna small talk
**Scene:** sauna bench, towel, ladle in hand. Steam visible if possible.
**Shot:** static, respectful framing (shoulders up).

1. Sits in silence. A long, comfortable silence. 4–5 full seconds.
2. Without looking at camera, raises the ladle slightly:
   > **"Heitänks lisää löylyä?"**
3. Throws the water (sound matters here — get the hiss). Silence resumes.

**Edit:** hook "The only small talk Finns actually do"; caption the sentence + translation; punch "Learn sauna Finnish first. Priorities."

### E2. Mökki mode
**Scene:** dock or lakeshore, phone in hand.
**Shot:** starts on the lake (2s of pure landscape), pans to him.

1. Holds phone up, searching for signal. Gives up. Not upset — relieved:
   > **"Mökil ei oo nettii."**
2. Puts phone away. Looks at the lake. Long breath.
   > **"Tääl on niin rauhallista."**
3. Hold on the lake 3s. That's the outro.

**Edit:** hook "Finland shuts down in July. Here's where everyone went."; minimal captions — let the landscape carry it; punch "Learn cottage Finnish before you get invited. You will get invited."

## While he's mic'd up: the audio pickup list

Before wrapping each location, record clean audio (no acting, just clear voice, two speeds — natural and slightly slow) of every sentence used above plus:

- "Onks sul nälkä?" / "Onks tääl tilaa?" / "Mitä sä otat?"
- "Mä oon kipee." / "Onks teil mitään flunssaan?"
- "Mä etin uut kämppää." / "Sopiiks, et mul on kissa?"
- "Kato toi!" / "Nähään!" / "Mennään jo!"

These become the voice bank for future clips and can replace TTS in the "Could you
survive?" quiz series — native audio is a real quality upgrade there.
