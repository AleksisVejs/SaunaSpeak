# Win-back emails

Two audiences, two mails. Pulled from Admin → Pulse → Win-back, which now has a
tab for each and marks everyone written to so nobody gets the same mail twice.

**Send these as plain text from your own address, not through the app.** The
branded template is right for the daily reminder and wrong here: this is a
question, not a campaign, and a designed email reads as marketing and gets
skimmed. Plain text from a person gets replies.

**The reply is the product here, not the click.** You have ~100 users and no
idea why most of them left. A win-back that converts 8% and teaches you nothing
is worth less than five honest answers about what went wrong.

Bcc the block the panel copies. If you have the patience to send them
individually with a first name, do — it roughly doubles reply rate on a list
this small, and 26 people is one sitting.

---

## 1. Never started

Registered, never graded a single thing. They have no impression of the product
to overcome — only whatever stopped them before it began. Don't sell. Ask.

**Subject:** did something break?

```
Hi — you made a SaunaSpeak account a few days ago and never got started,
and I'm trying to work out why.

I'm the person who built it (it's just me), so I'm asking directly: was
something broken, confusing, or just not what you expected? Did the
confirmation email land? Genuinely any answer helps — "got distracted and
forgot" is useful too.

If you'd rather just try it, one session is about two minutes:
https://saunaspeak.com/session

Either way, thanks for signing up.
Aleksis
```

Why it's built this way:

- **"did something break?"** as a subject assumes fault on our side, which is
  the opposite of a nag and the reason it gets opened. It is also often true.
- **"it's just me"** is the single highest-leverage line. People ignore
  companies and answer humans.
- **The confirmation-email question is doing real work.** A chunk of this group
  may never have received or found it, and that is a bug you can fix, not a
  preference you have to accept.
- **"got distracted and forgot" is pre-approved** so the low-effort reply feels
  allowed. Most people won't compose a critique, but they will confirm a guess.
- **The link is second, and small.** Leading with it turns this back into
  marketing and costs you the answer.

## 2. Came once, never returned

Did one real session and stopped. Unlike group 1, these people have seen the
product — so the question is what was missing, not what was broken.

**Subject:** you did one session — what was missing?

```
Hi — you did a SaunaSpeak session and never came back, and I'd really like
to know what was missing.

I built this on my own, so there's no research team behind this email, just
me trying to work out whether it's worth continuing. Too slow? Too easy?
Too hard? Not what you wanted? One line is plenty, and blunt is better than
polite.

If it helps: your sentences are still there and five of them is a real
session — https://saunaspeak.com/session

Thanks either way.
Aleksis
```

Why it's built this way:

- **"what was missing" presupposes something was**, which is easier to answer
  than an open "any feedback?" — that question reliably gets nothing.
- **Three guesses (slow / easy / hard)** turn composition into selection. Same
  trick as above, and it works because most replies are one word.
- **"whether it's worth continuing" is honest and it is the reason this works.**
  It gives the reader a reason their reply matters. Use it once. It stops being
  true the moment it becomes a tactic.
- **"blunt is better than polite"** gives explicit permission for the criticism
  you actually need. Without it people either say something kind or say nothing.
- **No streak language, no "we miss you", no guilt.** They owe you nothing, and
  the ones who feel nagged unsubscribe instead of answering.
- **"five is a real session"** matches the daily reminder's framing, so the
  return ask stays small and consistent.

---

## Reading the replies

Expect roughly 5–15% to answer — 5 to 15 replies across both groups. That is a
small number and it is still more than the entire snapshot told you, because it
is the only data you have that explains *why* rather than *what*.

Two things to watch for:

- **The same sentence twice.** Any complaint that shows up in three replies is
  a real problem. One vivid complaint is one person.
- **Nobody mentions the AI chat.** If not one reply asks for more conversation
  practice, that is your answer on what to charge for, and it is worth more
  than any amount of further analysis of the export.

Reply to everyone who writes back, even the harsh ones. Especially the harsh
ones — they are the people who cared enough to tell you.
